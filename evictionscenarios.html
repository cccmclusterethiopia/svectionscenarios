Eviction Scenarios Overview

Eviction Scenario Sub-Scenario Description Stakeholders Actions
1. Rent-Related Eviction Inability to pay rent Tenant unable to pay rent due to financial hardship (delays or non-payment). CCCM, BoLSA, Protection Cluster
See actionsa) Negotiate with the landlord for more time.
b) Refer to Cash for Rent partner for support.
c) Refer tenant to BoLSA for further assistance.
Partial Payment Tenant makes partial payments but can't pay the full rent. CCCM, Protection Cluster, BoLSA
See actionsa) Assist in negotiating payment terms.
b) Refer to Cash Assistance partners for additional support.
Delayed Payment Tenant delays rent payment due to loss of income or delayed wages. CCCM, BoLSA
See actionsa) Discuss with the landlord for delayed payment.
b) Provide financial assistance through MPC or other programs.
2. Resource-Related Eviction Failure to share resources Tenant evicted for not sharing humanitarian aid received with property owner. CCCM, Protection Cluster, Local Authorities
See actionsa) Facilitate dialogue between tenant and property owner.
b) Raise awareness on the roles of humanitarian aid.
3. Owner Needs Property Owner reoccupying property Property owner needs the space for their own use or for family. CCCM, Local Authorities
See actionsa) Negotiate with the owner to allow a grace period for tenant relocation.
b) Assist tenant in finding alternative shelter.
4. Expired Agreement or No Agreement Informal or verbal agreement expired Tenant evicted because their informal agreement has expired or wasn't renewed. CCCM, Local Authorities, Property Owner
See actionsa) Document tenant rights and help negotiate new terms.
b) Assist in legal matters through Protection Cluster.
No written contract Tenant evicted without a formal contract, no terms in place for eviction. Local Authorities, CCCM
See actionsa) Help tenant understand tenant rights.
b) Provide legal aid to advocate for tenants without contracts.
5. Institutional Eviction Property reclaimed by public institution Institutions like schools or health facilities evict tenants to reclaim the space for original use. Local Authorities, Public Institutions
See actionsa) Coordinate with institutions to find alternative accommodation for tenants.
b) Assist in relocation efforts.
6. Land Ownership Disputes Disputes over ownership or control Eviction due to legal disputes over land or property ownership. Legal Authorities, Local Authorities
See actionsa) Mediate between the conflicting parties.
b) Assist in resolving land dispute through legal means.
7. Secondary Occupation/Returnee Conflicts Return of original owner or tenants Original owners or tenants return to reclaim the property from displaced persons. Local Authorities, CCCM
See actionsa) Provide support to displaced persons for alternative housing.
b) Facilitate dialogue for peaceful relocation.
8. Discrimination or Stigmatization Eviction based on ethnicity, religion, or social bias Eviction driven by discriminatory factors targeting specific IDP groups. Local Authorities, Protection Cluster
See actionsa) Raise awareness on anti-discrimination laws.
b) Support victimized tenants with protection services.
9. Gender-Based Violence (GBV) or Family Breakdown Domestic violence, separation, widowhood Eviction due to domestic violence, family disputes, or widowhood, leaving the tenant without shelter. Protection Cluster, Local Authorities
See actionsa) Provide GBV support services to affected individuals.
b) Facilitate safe housing options for victims of violence.
10. Overcrowding or Hosting Fatigue Host evicting IDPs due to resource strain Hosts evict IDPs due to overcrowding and strain on resources, or increased social tensions. Local Authorities, CCCM, Protection Cluster
See actionsa) Facilitate dialogue to resolve tension between hosts and IDPs.
b) Explore options for rent support or relocation assistance for the displaced.
11. Government/Authority-Driven Eviction Policy shifts or land use change Government evicts tenants due to changes in local policies, land use, or relocation initiatives. Government Authorities, CCCM
See actionsa) Work with government for fair relocation options.
b) Help affected people secure land or alternative shelter.
12. Urban Development / Land Reclamation Eviction for development projects Eviction driven by urban development or infrastructure projects (e.g., road construction, housing projects). Local Authorities, Government Agencies
See actionsa) Assist in relocation to designated areas.
b) Advocate for affected persons' rights through local governance and legal frameworks.
13. Host Community Pressure Pressure from neighbors or landlords to evict IDPs Hosts or neighbors pressure property owners to evict displaced persons. Local Authorities, CCCM
See actionsa) Address root causes of host-tenant tension through mediation.
b) Offer host communities resources or support to alleviate strain.
